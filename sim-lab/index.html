How to run scenarios

  • Check each student's history on the Sim Lab Tracker tab before the day.
  • Progression per student: 2× Tier 1 → 2× Tier 2 → alternate Tier 3 / Tier 2.
  • Aim for ~50% Tier 2, the rest split across Tier 1 and Tier 3.
  • Submit a form for every scenario — it auto-updates the tracker.

Before the day

Prep from the cohort's clinical schedule sheet — open the Sim Lab Tracker tab (the last tab). For each student it shows:

  • How many scenarios they've led
  • The categories they've led
  • The tiers (difficulty) they've led
  • The scenario codes they've led
  • The scenario codes they've seen overall — led and been a team member on

The three tiers

  • Tier 1 — Foundations. Simpler scenarios. The focus is a solid assessment with good flow.
  • Tier 2 — Critical thinking. More complex, with a few possible pathways. Still built on a good assessment, but adds choosing the right interventions and thinking critically.
  • Tier 3 — High acuity, low frequency. Cardiac arrest, cric, major trauma, and the like. Students need a strong XABC's to catch life threats, the right skills at the right time, and good team leadership — staying organized and delegating when appropriate.

How each student should progress

Each individual student (not the group) should move through the tiers in this order:

  1. Two Tier 1 scenarios
  2. Two Tier 2 scenarios
  3. Then alternate the rest: one Tier 3, one Tier 2, and so on

Target mix by the end: about 50% of the scenarios a student led should be Tier 2, with the other half split between Tier 1 and Tier 3 — not mostly in either one. Use the tracker sheet to pick scenarios that hold this ratio.

Categories

Each student's categories are tracked too. Duplicates are fine, but the spread helps you see what kinds of calls a student has had — try to diversify them.

Fill out a form for every scenario

Submit a form (the Cohort links at the top of this page) for each scenario run. It records who led and who was on the team, and submitting it automatically updates that cohort's Sim Lab Tracker — no manual entry needed.

Each form also has a few performance rubric sections to note how the student did. These are optional, but filling them in helps other instructors see where a student's strengths and weaknesses lie.
